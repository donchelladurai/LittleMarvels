Support for families & carers.

Caring for a child with additional support needs can be exhausting and isolating. The whole family deserves a community of its own.

We see the parents and carers, the brothers and sisters, the grandparents who step in. We see the practical load and the quiet emotional one.

Our family-facing programmes exist to reduce isolation, share practical know-how, and give families space to be honest about hard days as well as the wins.

Image — coffee morning
Programmes for families

What's on offer.

Coffee Mornings

Regular, low-pressure gatherings for parents and carers. Tea, biscuits, kind ears, and the unsaid relief of being among people who get it.

Support Groups

Safe, facilitated spaces for families to share challenges, mark successes, and build the kind of friendships that hold up over time.

Caregiver Workshops & Training

Practical sessions on communication strategies, behaviour support, navigating school systems, sensory regulation, and more — taught by practitioners who do the work.

Home-Based Outreach

For families who can't easily access services in person — we'll come to you. Practical support, signposting, and someone in your corner.

Carer Wellbeing Sessions

Carers' wellbeing is our wellbeing too. Sessions focused on rest, boundaries, and building back the reserves it takes to keep going.
